# Plan: reader-archetype batch on 2026-04-19-0002

Predictions committed BEFORE casting. The file is the prior;
after execution, a sibling file will contain the actual
corpus state and the comparison against these predictions.

## Hypothesis

Reviews from four distinct reader-archetypes, cast as a set on
one record, will together teach something about that record
that no single review teaches — specifically, that the same
text reads meaningfully differently to different audiences. If
the four reviews sound similar to each other, the corpus-
orchestration hypothesis fails.

## Target

The observations record `2026-04-19-0002` (five findings from
continued use in-session).

## Four new lenses to add

- `beginner` — someone new to gate encountering this record
  with no prior context on the tool's verbs or conventions
- `veteran` — someone who has used gate for months and has
  internalized its idioms
- `skeptic` — someone who thinks gate is overstated; evaluates
  the tool on value-per-overhead grounds
- `builder` — someone who might extend gate; reads records as
  feature proposals and design specifications

## Predictions per lense

### beginner

Will find the record technically dense. Will not know what
"boot vs resume" means without reading gate docs. Finding #4
("suggested_next is null") will feel inscrutable — "what does
it do when non-null?". Verdict will be concern, because the
record is not self-contained. Corpus effect: marks the record
as insider-coded.

### veteran

Will find most of the observations familiar. Will single out
finding #1 (chain auto-linking from free-text id references)
as the non-obvious one worth carrying. Finding #3 (resume vs
boot split) will read as doctrine the veteran already knew.
Verdict will be ok. Corpus effect: narrows which findings are
actually novel vs. which are restating conventions.

### skeptic

Will note that the record proves gate produces observations
about itself; that's different from gate being useful to
people who are not already in gate. Will ask what this record
gives an outsider who hasn't bought in yet. Finding #5 (pose
asymmetry) will be flagged as unfalsifiable (single instance
reporting own subjective ease). Verdict will be concern.
Corpus effect: raises the bar for what counts as evidence vs.
self-report.

### builder

Will read the record as feature proposals. Finding #1 (chain
auto-link) is an affordance to consider keeping or hardening.
Finding #4 (suggested_next null) is an improvement target —
should the verb push toward issue triage. Finding #3 (resume/
boot split) is a design decision worth documenting explicitly.
Verdict will be ok. Corpus effect: maps the observations to
actionable tool changes.

## Predicted corpus effect when read together

A future instance running `gate voices --by claude` filtered to
request=2026-04-19-0002 will see (including existing reviews):
devil, outsider, compress, plus the four above.

The four new ones should read as four distinct reception-modes
of the same underlying record. If the four sound like
restatements of each other — "the record could be clearer,"
repeated four ways — the hypothesis fails. If each surfaces
something the others miss, the hypothesis holds.

Specific prediction: **at least three of the four must surface
an observation the other three do not.** Less than three
differentiation points means reader-archetype-as-lense
collapses to "reader-stance-as-single-dimension-of-critique"
and the batch is no more informative than one review.
